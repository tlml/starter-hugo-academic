abstract: The proton spectrum in the kinetic energy range 0.1 to 200 GeV was measured
  by the Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer (AMS) during space shuttle flight STS-91 at an
  altitude of 380km. Above the geomagnetic cutoff the observed spectrum is parameterized
  by a power law. Below the geomagnetic cutoff a substantial second spectrum was observed
  concentrated at equatorial latitudes with a flux /åisebox-0.5ex~70m$^-2$s$^-1$sr$^-1$.
  Most of these second spectrum protons follow a complicated trajectory and originate
  from a restricted geographic region.
